Project Overview

1.1 Background of the Study

The Cleaning Services Management System (CSMS) is sophisticated software designed to revolutionize the operational efficiency and organization of cleaning service businesses. This user-friendly system offers features to streamline cleaning operations, optimizing task management for booking and billing, customer management, employee scheduling, and inventory control.

In contrast to traditional paper-based methods, the CSMS offers a centralized platform for the systematic and secure storage of vital data concerning customers, cleaning staff, and cleaning projects. This transition from manual processes to digital record-keeping minimizes errors and expedites data management, significantly improving overall workflow.

1.2 Statement of the Problems

The effectiveness of a CSMS hinges on the efficiency of its subsystems. This study examines problems encountered within five key subsystems:

Employee Scheduling

Manual scheduling leads to inefficiencies, scheduling conflicts, and suboptimal workforce allocation. Difficulty tracking employee availability contributes to errors.

Payroll Management

Time-consuming and error-prone manual data entry for timesheets and payroll processing leads to inaccurate wage calculations and financial risks.

Customer Management

Difficulty managing customer information with manual processes leads to errors, inconsistent communication, and lack of scalability.

Inventory Management

Manual recording processes are time-consuming, prone to errors, and lack real-time updates. Lack of visibility leads to stockouts and inefficiencies.

Booking & Billing

Manual scheduling and billing processes are inefficient. Inaccuracies in billing and payment processing lead to financial losses and customer dissatisfaction.

Problem Statement

Employee Scheduling Problems

  • Manual scheduling processes lead to inefficiencies, such as scheduling conflicts and suboptimal workforce allocation
  • Difficulty in tracking and managing employee availability contributes to errors and missed opportunities
  • Reduced operational effectiveness due to inefficiencies and potential compliance issues
  • Decreased employee satisfaction and morale

Payroll Management Problems

  • Time-consuming and error-prone manual data entry for timesheets and payroll processing
  • Inaccurate wage calculations due to manual processes
  • Risk of financial losses due to overpayments, underpayments, and non-compliance
  • Decreased employee satisfaction due to inaccurate wage calculations and delays

Customer Management Problems

  • Difficulty in managing customer information due to manual processes
  • Inconsistent communication methods hinder effective communication
  • Lack of scalability and adaptability limits growth potential
  • Decreased customer satisfaction due to errors, delays, and communication issues

Project Objectives

1.3.1 General Objective

The main objective of the Cleaning Services Management System (CSMS) is to revolutionize the operational efficiency, organization, and customer satisfaction within cleaning service businesses at Busy Hands Cleaning Services Inc. This system aims to streamline crucial aspects of cleaning operations, including booking and billing, customer management, employee scheduling, and inventory control.

1.3.2 Specific Objectives

  1. Develop automated and streamlined workflows for task assignment, scheduling, payroll, and inventory management
  2. Foster seamless collaboration between employees and management with a dedicated communication channel
  3. Implement robust security protocols to safeguard sensitive data, ensuring compliance with regulations
  4. Design a secure database to store employee attendance, customer information, and inventory data
  5. Create algorithms for efficient employee assignment and accurate payroll calculations
  6. Develop a user-friendly platform offering easy clocking for employees and efficient data management
  7. Create a dedicated customer support line for prompt issue resolution
  8. Design a system for restricting bookings on specific dates to manage cleaning service availability

Scope and Delimitation

System Scope Delimitation
Employee Scheduling Create, manage, and edit accounts; assign bookings; view schedules Initially focuses on scheduling availability (leaves in future iterations)
Payroll Management Automate salary calculation; configure payroll components; secure access Will not handle tax calculations or direct fund transfers
Customer Management Centralize client data; communication channels; appointment scheduling Limited to Busy Hands' operational area
Inventory Management Digital inventory management; tracking cleaning supplies; stock reports Focuses solely on cleaning supplies inventory
Booking & Billing Display services; notify bookings; accept bookings; display receipts Confined to enhancing Busy Hands' booking and billing system

System Components

Functional Requirements

Employee Scheduling System

  • The system must allow the admin to create an account for company security
  • Admins must manage employees' payroll information and payslips
  • Admins must edit payroll information and view employees' attendance
  • Performers must create and save login information and record working hours

Payroll Management System

  • The system must allow the admin to create secure accounts
  • Admins must manage payroll information and edit details
  • The system should automatically calculate wages and display performance metrics
  • Timekeeping functionality and report generation capabilities
